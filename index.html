Energy Resources and Electricity

Science Lesson

Welcome to the Lesson

In this lesson we will learn about energy resources, renewable and nonrenewable resources, how fossil fuels are formed, the importance of electricity and how electricity is generated.



Oil and Water

Oil and water are natural resources used to generate energy. Some resources are renewable and some are nonrenewable.

Oil

Oil is a nonrenewable resource. It forms from the remains of marine organisms over millions of years.

Water

Water is a renewable resource because it can be replaced naturally through the water cycle.

Conservation of Oil and Water

Oil forms very slowly and water is essential for life. Therefore we must conserve both resources.

Reduce Oil Use

Using public transportation instead of private vehicles helps reduce oil consumption.

Avoid Wasting Water

We should avoid wasting or polluting water because it is very important for life.

Use Water Carefully

Farmers should grow plants that need less water for irrigation.

Formation of Fossil Fuels

Fossil fuels form from the remains of marine organisms over millions of years.

Marine Organisms

Marine organisms lived in ancient seas.

Buried Under Sediments

Their remains were buried under sediments and rocks.

Heat and Pressure

Heat and pressure turned these remains into fossil fuels.

Why Electricity Is Important

Electricity is used in homes, schools, hospitals and factories.

Lighting

Electricity lights homes and streets.

Appliances

Refrigerators, washing machines and fans need electricity.

Devices

Computers and many devices need electricity every day.

How Electricity Is Generated

Electricity is generated in power plants using fossil fuels.

1 Fuel is burned

2 Water turns into steam

3 Steam turns turbine

4 Turbine turns generator

5 Electricity reaches homes

Conserving Electricity

We must conserve electricity to save energy resources.

Turn Off Lights

Turn off lights when they are not needed.

Unplug Devices

Unplug electrical devices when they are not used.

Alternative Ways

Use candles and simple tools if electricity is not available.

Conclusion

Oil is a nonrenewable resource and water is renewable. Electricity is very important in our daily lives and we must conserve it.
