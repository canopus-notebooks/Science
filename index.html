Energy Resources and Electricity

Science lesson about energy resources and electricity

Energy Resources

Humans use natural resources to produce energy. Some resources can be replaced naturally and are called renewable resources, while others take millions of years to form and are called non-renewable resources.

Oil

Oil is a nonrenewable resource. It forms deep underground from the remains of marine organisms.

Water

Water is a renewable resource and can be used to generate electricity in hydroelectric power stations.

Formation of Fossil Fuels

Fossil fuels such as oil and natural gas are formed from the remains of marine organisms over millions of years.

Marine Life

Marine organisms lived in ancient seas.

Buried Under Rocks

After they died their remains were covered by sediments.

Heat and Pressure

Over millions of years heat and pressure transformed them into fossil fuels.

Importance of Electricity

Lighting Homes

Electricity provides lighting for homes, schools and streets.

Appliances

Many appliances such as refrigerators, fans and washing machines use electricity.

Devices

Computers, tablets and phones depend on electricity.

How Electricity Is Generated

In power plants fossil fuels such as oil, coal or natural gas are burned to produce electricity.

Step 1

Fuel is burned in a power plant.

Step 2

Water is heated until it becomes steam.

Step 3

Steam turns a turbine.

Step 4

The turbine spins a generator to produce electricity.

Conserving Electricity

Electricity is very important in our daily lives. Therefore we must conserve it and avoid wasting energy.

  • Turn off lights when not needed.
  • Unplug devices when they are not used.
  • Use electricity wisely.
  • Use natural light during the day.
