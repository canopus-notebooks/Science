Welcome to the Session

In this session, we will learn about pollution in big cities, the harms of burning fossil fuels, acid rain, global warming, how to reduce pollution, and why we must conserve fossil fuels.



Environmental Problems in Big Cities

Big cities suffer from many environmental problems because of factories, cars, agricultural activities, and the heavy use of fossil fuels.

Smog

Smog produced from burning fuels pollutes the air in many big cities.

Pesticides

Pesticides used in farms may reach canals and rivers and pollute water and soil.

Chemicals

Chemicals used in factories may pollute the air, water, and soil.

Effects of Air Pollution on Human Health

Air pollution affects people’s health and may cause serious damage to the body, especially the eyes, lungs, and respiratory system.

  • Smog causes irritation of human eyes.
  • Smog causes irritation of the lungs.
  • Small particles in polluted air may damage the respiratory system.
  • Breathing polluted air is harmful to human health.

Burning Fossil Fuels and Pollution

Fossil fuels such as coal, oil, and natural gas are burned to generate electricity and provide energy for homes, schools, factories, cars, and trains.

Burning Fuel

Burning fossil fuels provides energy, but it also pollutes the environment.

Generating Electricity

Fossil fuels are used in power stations to generate electricity.

Environmental Harm

Burning fossil fuels increases pollution and releases harmful gases.

Acid Rain and Global Warming

Burning fossil fuels releases carbon dioxide and other harmful gases. These gases may lead to acid rain and global warming.

Acid Rain

Acid rain harms trees, fish, lakes, soil, and even some rocks used for building.

Global Warming

Carbon dioxide forms a layer in the atmosphere that traps heat and raises Earth’s temperature.

  • Acid rain may kill fish and damage trees.
  • Acid rain changes the chemical nature of lakes and soil.
  • Global warming increases Earth’s temperature.
  • Global warming causes climate change.

How to Reduce Acid Rain and Global Warming

The best solution is to conserve energy. When people reduce energy use, the amount of burning fossil fuel decreases, so pollution also decreases.

  • Reduce the use of energy whenever possible.
  • Less energy use means less fossil fuel burning.
  • Less burning means less carbon dioxide in the air.
  • Conserving energy keeps the Earth cleaner.

Conserving Fossil Fuels

Fossil fuels should be conserved because they are formed over millions of years, they are limited, and they cannot be replaced quickly.

Use Bicycles

Walking or using bicycles instead of cars helps conserve fossil fuels.

Turn Off Lights

Turning off lights when not needed helps save energy and reduce fuel use.

Use Renewable Energy

Water, wind, and solar energy can replace fossil fuels in many uses.

Renewable and Nonrenewable Resources

Energy resources can be classified into renewable and nonrenewable resources.

Renewable Resources

Solar energy, water, wind energy, wood, and charcoal made from wood.

Nonrenewable Resources

Coal, gasoline, oil, and natural gas.

Think Like a Scientist

Scientists ask questions, make claims, give evidence, and explain their ideas clearly.

  • Question: Where does the fuel we use every day come from?
  • Claim: Some fuels come from fossil fuels and some come from renewable resources.
  • Evidence: Coal, oil, and natural gas are nonrenewable, while water and wind are renewable.
  • Scientific Explanation: Fossil fuels are formed over millions of years, but renewable resources can be replaced naturally.

Conclusion

In this session, we learned that burning fossil fuels causes pollution, acid rain, and global warming. We also learned that conserving energy and using renewable resources help protect the Earth and conserve fossil fuels.
